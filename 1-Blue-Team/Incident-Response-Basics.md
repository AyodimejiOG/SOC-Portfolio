# Incident Response Basics  
*TryHackMe – SOC Analyst Level 1*

## Overview

This lab introduced the structured process of incident response. Responding to security incidents requires clear procedures to reduce damage and restore normal operations efficiently.

I gained practical understanding of how incidents are handled from detection to recovery.

---

## Objectives

- Understand the stages of incident response
- Learn containment and eradication strategies
- Recognise the importance of documentation

---

## The Incident Response Lifecycle

### 1. Preparation
- Establish policies
- Train staff
- Deploy monitoring tools

### 2. Identification
- Detect unusual activity
- Validate alerts
- Confirm whether an incident has occurred

### 3. Containment
- Isolate affected systems
- Prevent lateral movement
- Limit business impact

### 4. Eradication
- Remove malicious files
- Patch vulnerabilities
- Reset compromised credentials

### 5. Recovery
- Restore systems
- Monitor for reinfection

### 6. Lessons Learned
- Document findings
- Improve detection rules
- Strengthen security controls

---

## Key Lessons

Incident response is not panic-driven — it is structured and methodical.

Clear communication and documentation are just as important as technical skills.

---

## Reflection

This lab showed me how critical structured processes are during a cyber incident. Without a defined response plan, organisations risk extended downtime and greater financial loss.

It strengthened my understanding of defensive strategy and the importance of teamwork within a SOC environment.
