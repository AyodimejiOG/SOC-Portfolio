## What is a SOC?

A Security Operations Center (SOC) is a dedicated team responsible for continuously monitoring and improving an organisation’s security.

The SOC operates 24/7 to:
- Monitor security alerts
- Investigate suspicious activity
- Respond to incidents
- Escalate threats when necessary
- Maintain visibility across systems and networks

It acts as the defensive backbone of an organisation’s cybersecurity strategy.

---

## Key Roles Within a SOC

### Tier 1 – SOC Analyst (Level 1)
- Monitors incoming alerts
- Performs initial triage
- Identifies false positives
- Escalates confirmed threats

### Tier 2 – SOC Analyst (Level 2)
- Conducts deeper investigations
- Performs log correlation
- Analyses attack patterns
- Recommends containment strategies

### Tier 3 – Threat Hunter / Senior Analyst
- Proactively hunts for hidden threats
- Develops detection rules
- Improves defensive capabilities

---

## Key Concepts Learned

### 1. Alert Triage
Not every alert is a real threat. Analysts must determine:
- Is this malicious?
- Is this suspicious?
- Is this a false positive?

This process prevents unnecessary escalation and reduces alert fatigue.

### 2. Incident Escalation
When a threat is confirmed, it follows a structured escalation path to ensure quick containment and minimal business impact.

### 3. Continuous Monitoring
A SOC never stops operating. Cyber threats do not follow business hours, making 24/7 monitoring critical.
